# Content, Refresh, and Information-Gain Asset

**Authority:** SEO BIBLE 2026 Parts 1, 3, 4, 7, 9, and 11.  
**Evidence rule:** Word count and header-count prescriptions are prohibited as ranking claims. Study-specific observations remain scoped to their studies.

## Page-existence gate

A brief must state why the page deserves to exist. Acceptable information gain includes first-party data, original examples, expert experience, screenshots, tools, comparison data, proprietary frameworks, or a materially clearer answer to an unresolved user need.

## Refresh decision

Classify existing content as:

- OPTIMIZE: limited changes such as links, metadata, calls to action, and FAQ improvements.
- UPGRADE: substantive statistics, examples, screenshots, sections, or structured data.
- REWRITE: major replacement reserved for high-value pages.
- CONSOLIDATE: merge pages that compete for the same intent.
- LEAVE_ALONE: no evidence of material benefit from change.

Use GSC and analytics trends, commercial value, links, impressions, conversions, seasonality, and internal-link role. Never delete a page solely because traffic is low.

## Structure

- Answer the primary question early.
- Use standalone sections that remain intelligible when extracted.
- Put the answer close to the heading.
- Use tables and lists when they genuinely improve comparison or comprehension.
- Do not fragment one intent into many thin pages.

## Prohibited claims

- Word count is a ranking factor.
- Exactly one H1 is required for rankings.
- Keyword density targets are ranking requirements.
- Changing only the publication date constitutes a meaningful update.
