# Regional Search Engines

Use this file when international SEO work targets markets where Google is not the only meaningful search engine.

## General Rule

Do not assume a Google-first implementation is sufficient for every market. Start with the target market, dominant search platforms, language, hosting constraints, legal constraints, and local user behavior.

## Bing

Considerations:

- Verify Bing Webmaster Tools separately when available.
- Submit XML sitemaps directly.
- Review robots.txt and meta robots behavior carefully; do not rely on blocked pages to communicate noindex.
- Validate IndexNow support for eligible sites.
- Check rich result and structured data eligibility independently from Google.

## Baidu

Considerations:

- China-focused sites may need local hosting, fast regional access, simplified Chinese localization, and regulatory review.
- Avoid relying on heavy JavaScript for critical content.
- Use clear HTML links and static-rendered important content where possible.
- Validate Chinese-language metadata, headings, and structured content with native review.

## Yandex

Considerations:

- Use Yandex Webmaster when relevant.
- Review regional targeting and language signals.
- Ensure technical crawlability without relying on Google-specific assumptions.
- Consider market and legal constraints before targeting Russia or surrounding regions.

## Naver

Considerations:

- South Korea search behavior often includes portal, blog, local, shopping, and knowledge surfaces.
- Content format and platform presence may matter as much as traditional web pages.
- Use Korean localization and market-native content review.

## Quality Gate

- The International & Multilingual SEO Agent must identify the dominant search platforms for each target market.
- Regional guidance must be treated as market-specific and validated with local expertise when business-critical.
