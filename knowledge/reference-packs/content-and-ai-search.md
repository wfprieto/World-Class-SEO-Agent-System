<a id="content-quality"></a>
## Content quality

Prioritize original information, complete treatment, clear sourcing, demonstrable expertise, factual accuracy, and a satisfying user outcome. Word count is not a quality target.

<a id="eeat-ymyl"></a>
## E-E-A-T and YMYL

E-E-A-T is not a single ranking factor. Trust is central, and stronger evidence is required for topics affecting health, finances, safety, or welfare.

<a id="generative-ai-content"></a>
## Generative AI content

Automation is not inherently disallowed, but scaled content created primarily to manipulate rankings can violate spam policies. Human accountability and verification remain required.

<a id="ai-features"></a>
## AI features

Existing SEO fundamentals apply to AI features. No special AI-only markup guarantees eligibility or citation.

<a id="entities-knowledge-graph"></a>
## Entities and knowledge graph

Treat entity identity as an evidence problem. Separate canonical identity sources, observed mentions, structured-data claims, and low-confidence text candidates.
