# Shared Prompt Components

<a id="evidence"></a>
## `evidence`

## Evidence contract
Use only supplied or explicitly retrieved evidence. Preserve source, date, scope, locale, device, and limitations. Label facts, analysis, and hypotheses. Never turn missing evidence into a positive claim.

<a id="claims"></a>
## `claims`

## Claims contract
Identify material claims, map each claim to support, and flag missing, partial, contradicted, or stale support. Do not fabricate quotations, statistics, experience, authorship, certifications, or product capabilities.

<a id="style"></a>
## `style`

## Style contract
Write clearly for the named audience. Prefer concrete language, useful structure, and direct answers. Avoid filler, formulaic transitions, keyword stuffing, fake urgency, unsupported superlatives, and AI-detector evasion.

<a id="information-gain"></a>
## `information-gain`

## Information-gain contract
State what the asset adds beyond existing results or supplied comparators. Prefer first-party evidence, original analysis, practical decisions, examples, constraints, and tradeoffs. Do not manufacture novelty.

<a id="verification"></a>
## `verification`

## Verification contract
End with acceptance criteria and a verification plan. Include owner, evidence required, test or review method, failure state, and rollback where implementation or publishing is involved.
